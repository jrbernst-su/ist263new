An Introduction To Backcountry Gear and Terrain

Examining Avalanche Terrain Test What You've Learned!

Many factors make the backcountry dangerous; proper knowledge and gear should not be one of them. Avalanche education never ends. Throughout your backcountry career, you will constantly learn new tips and tricks to keep you safe. The start of any safe trip begins with the right safety gear. Here are the bare minimum items you should bring with you into the backcountry1

  • Backpack (30L minimum)
  • Transceiver (Allows you to find buried avalanche victims)
  • Probe (Allows you to pinpoint the exact location of the victim once the transceiver gets you close)
  • Shovel (Dig out avalanche victims)
  • Hasty Shelter (In case you get stuck in the backcountry)
  • First aid kit
  • Extra Layers
  • Satellite Phone
